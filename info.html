ETC — SNR calculator

Description

This straightforward ETC provides an estimated signal-to-noise ratio for any spectro-imager instrument. Its key attributes lie in its versatility, user-friendly interface, and diverse plotting options. The ETC is designed to accommodate any spectro-imager, and scientists can contribute new instruments or configurations on the shared spreadsheet for direct utilization within the tool.

The ETC GUI offers a quick overview of essential instrument parameters and constraints, along with insights into tradeoffs, potential optimizations and mitigation strategies. Plots dynamically adjust as parameters are modified.

Interest and Philosophy

Exposure Time Calculators are integral tools for optimizing observational strategies in astronomy, typically tailored to specific instruments. Generic ETC takes a different route: a broadly applicable, generic ETC built on a shared instrument database.

Beyond the nominal use of predicting the SNR of a source given the site, instrument and observation parameters, it allows analyzing the evolution of the SNR with any instrument parameter — useful to examine instrument efficiency, explore SNR evolution under different scenarios, and run trade studies. The current database already encompasses more than 20 instruments, some with several channels or configurations. Predictions have been cross-checked with ETC-42 on several spectrograph designs.

Outputs (this web app)

SNR visualization

The SNR plot offers a straightforward depiction of noise budgets concerning various variables that can be fine-tuned or mitigated to enhance instrument sensitivity:

  • Panel 1 — Noise budget. Noise from each source (Signal, Dark, Sky, RN, CIC, Extra) in electrons per pixel per exposure, plus the quadratic sum.
  • Panel 2 — Contributions stack. The mean electron-per-pixel value for each component (pre-stacking), scaled by the number of pixels used.
  • Panel 3 — SNR stack. The relative fractions of all noise sources per resolution element over N frames, and the resulting SNR.
  • Panel 4 — Surface brightness limits. log10 of the SNR=5 limiting surface brightness per pixel, per resolution element, and per source.

Parameters

All instrument parameters can be used as the x-axis to analyze their impact on SNR. Their units are described in the instrument spreadsheet (as remarks) and in slider tooltips.

Source

  • Flux (F): source surface brightness in erg/cm²/s/arcsec²/Å.
  • Sky (S): sky background illumination in erg/cm²/s/arcsec²/Å.
  • Source extension (σx): spatial extent of the source in arcsec (σ).
  • Source's line width (σλ): spectral extent of the source / emission line in Å.

Observing strategy

  • Observed wavelength (λ): effective wavelength in nm.
  • Exposure time (texp): individual frame exposure time in seconds.
  • Total acquisition time (Ttot): total acquisition time in hours.
  • Atmospheric transmission (Atm%): %/100.
  • λ Stack: number of spectral pixels stacked.
  • Readout time: time between exposures (seconds).

Instrument design

  • Collecting area (A): physical collecting area in m².
  • Plate scale (P): pixel plate scale in ″/pix.
  • Throughput (T%): instrument throughput at effective wavelength (not including detector QE and atmosphere).
  • Spatial resolution (Rmask, Rdet): at the mask and at the detector.
  • Throughput FWHM (imager mode): bandpass FWHM in Å.

Spectrograph design

  • Spectral resolution (Rλ): λ[Å] / dλ[Å].
  • Slit width (Sw): slit / slice / fiber width in ″.
  • Slit length (Sl): slit / slice / fiber length in ″.
  • Dispersion (d): Å/pix at the detector.
  • Bandwidth: total spectrograph bandwidth in Å.
  • IFS: convert the slit/fiber design into a slicer / fiber-IFS. The flux normally lost at the slit is transferred to neighboring fibers/slices.

Detector

  • Quantum efficiency (QE%).
  • Dark current (D): e-/pix/hour.
  • Read noise (RN): e-/pix.
  • Cosmic-ray pixel loss (CR%): fraction of pixels lost per second.
  • Extra background (B): additional straylight in e-/pix/hour.
  • EM gain (G): EMCCD amplification gain.
  • CIC: clock-induced charge for EMCCDs (e-/pix).
  • γ-Threshold: enables EMCCD photon-counting mode.

Important features

  • Simplicity to add any instrument (just edit the shared spreadsheet).
  • Real-time computation in the browser.
  • Works for almost any spectrograph or spectro-imager: slit, slitless, prism, fiber IFS, slicer IFS.
  • Visualize the evolution of (noise, contributions, SNR, limiting surface brightness) with any parameter — for tradeoff analysis or instrument/observation/reduction optimization.
  • Easy instrument comparison.

Noise budget — formulas

Sky and signal

Sky and signal contributions are first converted from erg/cm²/s/asec²/Å to photons/cm²/s/sr/Å (continuum unit):

CU = F / [ (h·c/λ) · (π / (180·3600))² ]

The wavelength dependency in the formula is explicit. Flux is expressed per Å with a Gaussian profile so that the user can simulate either a continuum or an unresolved line.

Both contributions are then converted into electrons per pixel:

S [e-/pix/exp] = SCU · Swstr · dÅ/pix · texp · Atm% · A · T% · QE%

Other contributions

Dark current and straylight are given in e-/pix/hour:

D [e-/pix/exp] = De-/pix/h · texp / 3600

CIC (clock-induced charges, only relevant for EMCCDs) is already in e-/pix/exp. Read noise is also in e-/pix/exp.

Conversion to noise

Each contribution is then converted to noise by taking the square root, accounting for the effective number of frames and the resolution element size:

Ncontrib = sqrt(contrib · ENF · Nimages · Sizeres)

The effective number of frames is:

Nimages = (Ttot,s / (texp + tRO)) · (1 − CR%)

For electron-amplified CCDs (EMCCDs):

  • Read noise is divided by the amplification gain: RN' = RN / G.
  • An excess noise factor of √2 is used to account for the stochastic amplification (unless photon-counting / thresholding is applied).

Adding an instrument

To add a new instrument, edit the shared Google spreadsheet ("In progress" tab). Once all characteristics are indicated, the instrument is moved to the main tab and immediately becomes available in this app (use the Reload button under "Data source" in the controls panel).
